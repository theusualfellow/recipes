Description

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish that’s rich with spices, tender meat (usually chicken, mutton, or beef), and aromatic herbs. Originating from South Asia, it’s a one-pot dish that combines layers of cooked rice and marinated meat, typically cooked in a sealed pot to infuse all the flavors together. This hearty dish is perfect for special occasions, and is often served with raita, boiled eggs, or a side salad.

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 green cardamom pods
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste

For the Marinade:

  • 500g (1 lb) chicken (or mutton/beef), cut into pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Gravy:

  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (optional, for garnish)

For Layering:

  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 cup saffron milk (a pinch of saffron soaked in warm milk)
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (optional, for garnish)
  • Fresh cilantro and mint leaves for garnishing

Instructions

1. Prepare the Rice:

  1. Wash the Basmati rice thoroughly and soak it for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil and add the bay leaves, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and cumin seeds.
  3. Once the water is boiling, add the soaked rice and cook until it's 70-80% cooked (the rice should still have a bite to it). Drain the rice and set it aside.

2. Marinate the Meat:

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt to make the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken (or mutton/beef) pieces and coat them evenly with the marinade.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight for deeper flavor).

3. Prepare the Gravy:

  1. Heat 2 tbsp ghee (or oil) in a large pot or pan over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and sauté until they are golden brown.
  2. Add the ginger garlic paste and sauté for another minute, until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices (about 5 minutes).
  4. Next, add cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt to the tomatoes, and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the marinated meat to the gravy and cook for about 10-15 minutes until the meat is browned and cooked through. Stir in fresh cilantro and mint leaves, and remove from heat.

4. Layer the Biryani:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) or prepare to cook on the stovetop using a heavy-bottomed pot.
  2. In a large pot, spread a thin layer of the cooked meat and gravy at the bottom.
  3. Layer half of the cooked rice on top of the meat, then drizzle a little ghee and saffron milk over the rice.
  4. Repeat the layering with the remaining meat, rice, ghee, and saffron milk.
  5. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with dough to trap the steam and cook the biryani.

5. Cook the Biryani:

  1. Cook the biryani on low heat for 25-30 minutes, either in the oven or on the stovetop (using a heavy pot or tawa underneath to prevent direct heat). This will allow all the flavors to meld.

6. Serve:

  • Once the biryani is ready, fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with fried onions, fresh cilantro, and mint leaves.
  • Serve with raita, a side salad, or boiled eggs for a complete meal.
